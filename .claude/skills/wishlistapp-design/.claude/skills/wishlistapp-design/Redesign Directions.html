WishlistApp · Web redesign

Three directions to make gifting feel good.

Today the web client is stock Bootstrap: a gray, admin-like surface that opens on a global users list and navigates by breadcrumb only. The product is actually warm and social — helping people get gifts they'll love. These three directions each give it a real identity and a clearer navigation model. Same features, same copy voice; very different feel.

Assumptions: web client; keep every current capability (public lists, items with price / priority / images, reserving & copying, profiles, admin); free to leave Bootstrap. Tell me what resonates and I'll build the winner into a full clickable prototype.

Navigation rethink (shared across all three)

WAS
Opens on “Users”. First screen is a flat list of everyone — confusing as a home.
NOW
Opens on “My Lists”. Your own wishlists are home; you act before you browse.
WAS
Breadcrumb-only. The top bar title is the only sense of place.
NOW
Persistent sections. My Lists · Discover (people) · Reserved (the booking feature, finally surfaced) + global search.
01

Cozy Registry

Warm · editorial

A thoughtful gift-registry feel: warm paper background, a serif display face (Newsreader) over a clean grotesk, generous whitespace and large product imagery. Terracotta + sage keep it warm without being childish. Best if the brand should feel personal and considered. Nav: centered pill tabs in a slim top bar (My Lists · Discover · Reserved), search + avatar on the right. Breadcrumb replaced by a big page title.

02

Calm Studio

Minimal · product

A premium, tool-like aesthetic (think Linear / Notion): near-monochrome with a single indigo accent, crisp 1px borders, a geometric sans (Manrope), tight spacing and a persistent left sidebar. Best if you want it to feel like a fast, modern app you live in. Nav: fixed left sidebar (Home · Discover · Reserved · Settings) with your profile pinned at the bottom; global search in the top bar; segmented Grid/List + sort. Keeps a small breadcrumb for depth.

03

Joy

Playful · social

Bright and friendly: soft lavender/mint wash, a rounded geometric sans (Plus Jakarta Sans), big rounded cards, vivid priority chips and a floating bottom dock. Most approachable / consumer-social. Best if the audience skews casual and the joy of gifting is the point.
